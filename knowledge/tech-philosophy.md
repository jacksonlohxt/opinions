# Technology & Product Philosophy

*Durable beliefs and evolving views — extracted from my writing.*
> Last updated: 2026-06-29
> Source: Apple Notes

## Durable Beliefs

- **Technology should adapt to how humans naturally think, not the other way around.** Rather than humans learning to communicate with computers (learning coding languages), we should remove that step — let humans type in natural language and have the system understand them at the human layer. [Note #9, 2026-06-29]
- **AI helps separate two dimensions of being human:** (1) the human part — compassion, tolerance for pain, determination after setbacks; (2) the intelligence part — pattern recognition, computation, analysis. AI can handle the second, leaving humans to focus on the first. [Note #12, 2026-06-29]
- **MOBA games (like Dota 2) and coding are fundamentally the same activity.** Both give you a set of resources and ask: how do you max-min those resources to achieve the best result? The strategic thinking transfers. [Note #12, 2026-06-29]
- **People need a "second brain" that deeply understands them.** The best product is one that is closely related to the user — it understands their context without judgment and creates clarity from their mental chaos. Apple keeps things simple to remove friction between user and product; a second brain should do the same for thinking. [Note #9, 2026-06-29]
- **Great startups apply already accepted societal behaviors to underserved domains.** We normalize online-first commerce and trust everywhere — except in domains like amateur sports coaching, which remain hyper-local and fragmented by luck. [Note #16, 2026-06-29]
- **Learning through depth beats speed.** Spending extended time staring at a problem or question leads to better memory retention than quick answers from ChatGPT or Google. The struggle itself embeds the knowledge — if you spent a long time on a question, you'll remember it better next time. [Note #9, 2026-06-29]
- **Mindmaps are the optimal human interface for complexity.** Visual mindmaps let users understand complex information at a glance — they're more effective than raw text or lists for comprehension. The goal is to let the user focus and understand with ease. [Note #10, 2026-06-29]
- **Specialize, don't generalize.** The support role (Pos5) in Dota succeeds by focusing on covering one specific weakness exceptionally well rather than trying to do everything competently. Hone in on one role and do it excellently — this applies far beyond gaming. [Note #9, 2026-06-29]
- **Parallel reasoning amplifies thinking.** Collaborative reasoning where multiple people contribute perspectives to pivot the trajectory of an idea produces better outcomes than solo thinking, which can be uncertain or self-manipulated. [Note #12, 2026-06-29]
- **Marketing is applied human psychology.** Effective marketing touches the psychology of human spirit — it's about understanding what makes people do what they do. This is what makes mass communication possible. [Note #12, 2026-06-29]

## Recurring Themes

- **Human layer vs machine layer.** A recurring two-part model: there is the human dimension (connection, emotion, experience, compassion) and the machine/intelligence dimension (computation, efficiency, AI). The goal is not to merge them but to let each do what it does best, with technology serving the human side. [Notes #9, #12, 2026-06-29]
- **Reducing friction.** Whether in product design (second brain app), community building (low-barrier social spaces), or learning (natural language over code), the recurring instinct is to remove the step between intention and action. [Notes #6, #9, #85, 2026-06-29]
- **Preparation as a discipline.** The idea that you constantly prepare — skills, content, positioning — ahead of time, so when the right opportunity arrives, you're already ready. Appears in the context of media preparation and career positioning. [Notes #10, #12, 2026-06-29]
