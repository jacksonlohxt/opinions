# Design Preferences & App Ideas

**Current synthesis:**
- Keep it simple — remove friction between user and product. The best product understands you without judgment.
- Freeform, unrestricted input is the most human way to write — don't guide, don't restrict, let thoughts flow.
- Mindmaps are the best way for humans to understand complexity — visual representation enables "stare and understand."
- Reduce to a single hook — humans have finite attention. Three items is "almost too many."
- Make things convenient and put in effort so the user doesn't have to — it signals care: "you make effort to make it easy for me."
- 25+ app ideas spanning second brain tools, AI agent architecture (Dota-style), community platforms, accessibility, commerce, and personal growth systems. Active cluster: knowledge management and AI agent design.

## Design Preferences

### Interface Philosophy
- **Keep it simple — remove friction between user and product.** Apple's approach is the model: think simple, reduce barriers. [Note 5]
- **Human-first design.** Integrate the system to work for humans, not make humans work for the machine. Remove the step of learning to code to communicate with computers — let people use natural language. [Note 5]
- **The best product is one deeply related to the user.** It should understand them without judgment. When a product is "fully relate you," it becomes the best thing ever. [Note 5]
- **Every interface step must earn its place.** Question whether an interface is needed at each step of a process — avoid overkill. [Note 5]

### Visual Design & Information Architecture
- **Mindmaps are the best way for humans to understand.** Visual representation of summarized ideas lets users "stare and understand." [Note 5, Note 10]
- **Segment information for easy eye tracking.** The core objective is call-to-action — if segmenting achieves that, it works. [Note 94]
- **Side-by-side comparison through clean interfaces** for making choices (e.g., coaching philosophies). [Note 16]
- **Specific examples make content stand out** — they transport the reader "to the place so I visual I there." [Note 101]

### Input & Workflow Design
- **Freeform, unrestricted input.** The most human-nature way to write: "roam around the brain and just think free and got thought then just write." No guided questions, no restrictions. [Note 5]
- **Input should work anytime, anywhere.** Don't limit by when or where the thought happens. [Note 5]
- **Follow the wave.** Work with the flow of high interest rather than fighting against it. Challenge workflows daily, pivot when needed. [Note 9, Note 12]
- **Compile and synthesize.** Write notes freely throughout the week, then compile and feed to AI for extraction of learnings. [Note 12]
- **Reduce to a single hook.** Humans have finite attention space — "how does make 3 item into 1." If there are 3 items, it's "almost too many." [Note 9]

### Accessibility & Inclusivity
- **Dynamic UI/UX for colourblind and elderly users.** [Note 5]
- **Guide senior citizens through unfriendly interfaces** using AI that understands what they want to do. [Note 5]
- **Voice-driven interfaces** for users facing language barriers (e.g., migrant workers completing onboarding). [Note 5]
- **Make things convenient and accessible.** Put in effort so the user doesn't have to — "it does feel to the person that you make effort to make it easy for me." [Note 94, Note 101]
- **Low-barrier spaces** — design for easy entry and participation. [Note 85]

### Tool & Environment Preferences
- **VS has an ugly UI** — preference for JetBrains IDEs (fewer plugins needed, cleaner look). [Note 128]
- **Vim keybinds are "bizarre" but very efficient once learned.** Use IDE with vim plugin rather than raw vim. [Note 128]
- **Linux is better for programming** due to package managers. [Note 128]
- **Practical tools over theory.** Learn the tools you use to write, debug, and archive code before diving into CS theory. [Note 128]
- **Minimalistic environment setup** is valued. [Note 142]
- **Use hyperlinks generously** — they provide easy access and signal effort: "it does feel to the person that you make effort to make it easy." [Note 101]

### Communication & UX Writing
- **Minimalism in communication.** Compress to essentials: "you really want to be able to actually convey and actually communicate out a particle." [Note 133]
- **Energy is finite.** Take small breaks; communication compresses energy into singular moments. [Note 133]
- **Empathise with the user.** Step into their shoes, make things "easier and funner." [Note 94]
- **Core objective of any communication = call to action.** [Note 94]

### Product Design Philosophy
- **"Great startups don't just build features; they apply already accepted societal behaviors to highly underserved domains."** [Note 16]
- **Remove friction from processes.** CoachLink: "We are removing the friction from athletic growth." [Note 16]
- **Trust is built through transparency** — e.g., explicitly explain why a match/recommendation makes sense. [Note 16]
- **Data integrity matters in UX.** Reviews should be authentic, non-duplicated, owner-only editable. [Note 44]

### Learning Design
- **ChatGPT as personal tutor, not unpaid labour.** Use it to explain concepts rather than do the work. [Note 128]
- **Learn by doing (practical first, theory later).** Harvard CS50x recommended because it "does not hold your hand." [Note 128]
- **Staring at a question longer leads to better memory retention** vs. immediately getting the answer. [Note 5]

## App Ideas

### Second Brain / Knowledge Management

1. **"Grey" App** — A second brain that extracts useful content from diary/journal entries written in fragmented, natural language. Core insight: the bottleneck is brain energy spent organizing — free that energy for thinking. [Note 5]

2. **"Alive" / Mindmap App** — Visual mindmap of your thoughts. Context-aware for meetings: type in what you're currently doing and it pulls relevant past notes/ideas. Goal: let users "stare and understand" via visual representation. [Note 5, Note 10]

3. **"Jack" App** — Diary → Gemini (large window for synthesis) → Codex (generates mindmap visual). Context-aware: pulls relevant info based on meeting topic (e.g., "ai hackathon qwen" → surfaces hackathon ideas). [Note 10]

4. **Weekly Note Compiler** — Write notes freely, compile weekly, feed to Gemini to extract learnings and patterns. [Note 12]

5. **An Easier Way to Remember Things** — Unspecified concept for memory aid. [Note 5]

6. **Finding the Moment When Inspiration Strikes** — Finding specific paths through campus without filming guide videos. [Note 5]

### AI Agent Architecture

7. **Dota 5-Man Agent Concept** — Apply MOBA team dynamics to AI coding agents. 3-stage game phases (early/mid/late), each with different playstyles. Drafting phase: analyze the problem, then select which agent formation (Split Push / Late Game / Win All Lanes) to deploy. [Note 5]

8. **Firstmate + 5-Man Structure** — Extend Firstmate with Dota-style roles: Pos 5 covers Pos 1's shortcomings rather than trying to do everything well. Specialization over generalization. [Note 5]

### Social & Community Platforms

9. **Podcast Community Platform** — A space for genuine conversations and belonging. Problem: after school (age 25-30+), people lose natural social opportunities. Work relationships are often superficial/motivated. Solution: recreate the "school feeling" — no agenda, just genuine connection. [Note 73, Note 85]

10. **80/20 Podcast** — Chat with people in tech about what they're building, the ideas driving them, and their journey. Already running (early days). [Note 127]

11. **Community Belonging Platform** — Podcast as a platform that sparks dialogue, builds community, and gives people the feeling of belonging even when daily life grows isolating. Call to action: get people to reach out more often. [Note 85]

### Accessibility & Inclusion

12. **healmyUX** — An app that guides senior citizens through unfriendly user interfaces. Tell an AI extension what you want to do and it helps you navigate. Add-ons: auto-fill forms with saved user info. [Note 5]

13. **Dynamic UI/UX for Colourblind & Elderly** — Adaptive interfaces that adjust for visual impairments and age-related needs. [Note 5]

### Commerce & Marketplaces

14. **CoachLink Baseball** — Digital infrastructure for online-first baseball coaching discovery. Players input skill level, position, budget, goals. Rule-based matching protocol surfaces relevant regional coaches with explicit "why this match" explanations. Clean side-by-side comparison. One-click video upload to connect with mentors. Hackathon MVP. [Note 16]

15. **Rainmaker** — Merchant loyalty/rewards platform. 600 "rainmakers" (influential bosses), 6% service charge on transactions (3% rebate to customers, 3% to platform). F&B focus. Pain points: merchants fear tech, fear data theft. [Note 88]

### Productivity & Workplace

16. **CRM Case System** — Auto-logging cases in CRM from meeting speech. Abstract to Teams, connect to input fields, configure AI for insights in your preferred style. [Note 5]

17. **Meeting Context App** — When you're in a meeting, the app pulls all relevant past notes/ideas for that topic. Mindmap-ready for further brainstorming. [Note 5]

18. **Schedule/Outreach App** — Schedule when to reach out to people, "robot it out and put myself out there." [Note 5]

### Lifestyle & Utility

19. **Inventory Management System for Gifts** — Track your basket of gifts according to friends and their birthdays. [Note 5]

20. **Networking Made Easy** — Connect with someone via NFC tag on your phone. Configurable for different settings (professional: LinkedIn only; social: private Instagram). Gentle reminder for receiver to download the app. [Note 5]

21. **Book Grab & Gojek via Telegram/WhatsApp** — Book rides through messaging apps. Integrations: GrabFood, Calendar, other shortcuts. [Note 5]

22. **Restaurant Finder** — Find a restaurant from a friend's Instagram post without location tags. Or identify an unknown dish and find places nearby that serve it. [Note 5]

23. **What to Cook with Fridge Ingredients** — Connected to Instagram/Facebook. Con: user friction to type in what's in their fridge. [Note 5]

24. **Exercise But Don't Push Too Hard** — Exercise app concept (undefined). [Note 5]

### Legal & Social Impact

25. **Family Law & Estate Management Platform** — Smart asset-probate orchestration. Families upload chaotic bundles of legal/financial documents → IDP extracts assets, debts, relationships → mapped to court filing fields. Includes emotional check-in tracker and client-lawyer call transcription. Hackathon idea. [Note 5]

26. **Migrant Worker Onboarding & Welfare Platform** — Multilingual, voice-driven interface in workers' native languages. Complete onboarding checklists, upload identity papers (auto-populate compliance forms), report wellbeing. Employer dashboard for paperwork bottlenecks. Family/NGO portal for verification. Hackathon idea. [Note 5]

### Personal Growth Systems

27. **"Quest" System** — Daily vlogs as routine, 90-day challenge, career switch into tech. Explore skills future-workforce. Seasons-based note organization (Japan seasons concept). Minimalistic environment setup. [Note 142]

28. **Seasons-Based Note Organization** — Organize notes according to different "seasons" (like Japan's distinct seasons), applied to content themes. [Note 142]
