## Project Options — Pick One

*You have 10 minutes to decide. Here are the two strongest, pulled from your notes. Come in ready to pitch either.*

### Option A: CoachLink Baseball

**Problem we're solving:**
SEA amateur baseball players have no way to find good coaches. The local pool is tiny. They're stuck watching unguided YouTube videos, getting intimidated, and quitting.

**Why judges would care:**
- Real market gap — Japan and Taiwan have world-class coaches, SEA has demand, but zero infrastructure connects them
- Clear unfairness narrative — society normalizes online trust for commerce, dating, work — but sports coaching is still luck-based
- Scalable — video analysis means no physical constraints, works across borders immediately
- Hackathon-appropriate scope — focused on the first connection, not building a full platform

**MVP (minimum that works):**
1. Player inputs: skill level, position, budget, goals
2. Rule-based matching shows relevant coaches from Japan/Taiwan
3. Side-by-side coach comparison (philosophy, style, video samples)
4. Single-click upload of swing/pitching video → connects to coach

**What we cut if time runs out:**
- Payment processing
- Real-time chat
- Coach profiles/bios (just show name + specialty + sample video)
- Physical clinic booking
- Mobile app — web-only

**Your key line (from your notes):**
*"Great startups apply already accepted societal behaviors to highly underserved domains."*

### Option B: Second Brain / Grey App

**Problem we're solving:**
People have ideas all the time but lose them. They show up to meetings empty-handed because none of their past thinking is accessible in the moment. Current tools force you to organize before you capture — that's backwards.

**Why judges would care:**
- Universal problem — every creative person, student, founder has this
- AI-native solution — extraction and surfacing is the core value, not just storage
- Privacy angle — personal knowledge base that stays local, understands YOU specifically
- Different from Notion/Obsidian — those are organizers, this is a retriever

**MVP (minimum that works):**
1. Free-form text input — just write when a thought hits, zero structure
2. Backend categorizes entries into buckets (idea, question, decision, knowledge gap)
3. Meeting mode: input context (topic, attendees) → surfaces top 3-5 relevant past entries
4. Daily digest: "here's what you've been thinking about this week"

**What we cut if time runs out:**
- Multi-AI provider support (pick one: Claude or Gemini)
- Mobile app — web PWA only
- Export/sharing features
- Calendar integration
- Voice input

**Your core insight (from your notes):**
*"The best product is one that is closely related to the user — it understands their context without judgment and creates clarity from mental chaos."*
